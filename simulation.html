Lesson 1: Probability & Simulations

We use models to build logical approximations that help us understand real situations.

Learning goals

By the end, you should be able to:

  • Explain the difference between theoretical and experimental probability.
  • Run a simulation and collect results fairly.
  • Compare results and judge how accurate your simulation is.

Helpful idea: More trials usually gives results closer to the theoretical probabilities.

Key words

Model: a simplified representation of a real situation.

Approximation: nearly correct, but not exact.

Validity: the model makes sense logically for the situation.

Quick check: When would a simulation be “not valid”?

Simulation Requirements

  1. Design: define Success and Failure, then find theoretical probabilities.
  2. Choose tools: pick a tool that matches the probabilities fairly.
  3. Run: do at least 50 trials and record results.
  4. Compare: experimental vs theoretical.
  5. Evaluate: one thing that worked well, and one improvement (validity/accuracy).

Useful sentence starters

Our simulation is valid because…

A limitation of our method is…

To improve accuracy, we could…

Our results are different from the theory because…
