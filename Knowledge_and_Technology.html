Knowledge & Technology (TOK Optional Theme)

This page curates key resources for exploring how technology shapes, expands, and challenges what we count as knowledge. Each section pairs a short video primer with a brief overview and a forthcoming visual organizer you can swap in once it’s ready.

Scope

Technology doesn’t merely transmit knowledge—it transforms the very scope of inquiry. From search algorithms to augmented‑reality labs, digital tools accelerate discovery, compress distance, and create new domains of data that didn’t exist before. This raises classic TOK questions about the limits of knowing at machine speed, the permanence of digital records, and whether quantity of information enhances or dilutes understanding.

Methods & Tools

Technological artifacts—from telescopes and particle accelerators to AI language models—act as both methods and mediators of knowledge. They extend sense perception, enable simulation, and crunch vast datasets, but they also embed assumptions (code, calibration, training data) that color the results. TOK asks: when is a model an accurate mirror and when is it a fun‑house reflection?

Ethics

Who benefits when knowledge is codified into apps, algorithms, or surveillance systems? Ethical reflection in this theme probes issues such as data privacy, algorithmic bias, intellectual property, and the digital divide. It challenges learners to weigh innovation against responsibility and to consider whose voices are amplified—or erased—by technological platforms.

Perspectives

Attitudes toward technology range from techno‑optimism to deep skepticism. This section surfaces cultural, historical, and disciplinary perspectives on how technology mediates knowledge claims—whether crowdsourced encyclopedias democratize expertise or whether echo‑chambers and deepfakes fragment shared reality.
