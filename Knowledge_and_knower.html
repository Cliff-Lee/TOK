TOK – Core Theme: Knowledge & the Knower

Scope

How do we distinguish personal knowledge—shaped by experience, culture, and identity—from shared knowledge that communities build over time? In this theme we map the territory of what it means to know, asking whether knowledge can ever be independent of the knower and probing the limits of certainty in everything from daily intuitions to scientific theories.

Perspectives

We never approach knowledge from a neutral vantage point. Our lenses— cultural, linguistic, ideological—shape the questions we ask and the evidence we trust. TOK invites you to examine how multiple perspectives can enrich (or fragment) the search for truth, and why stepping outside your own viewpoint is both challenging and illuminating.

Methods & Tools

From sensory perception and reasoned argument to digital note‑taking apps and AI assistants, we rely on a toolbox to build and organise what we know. TOK asks: How reliable are these methods? Do new technologies merely extend our cognition or fundamentally reshape what counts as knowledge? And how do language and emotion entwine with logic in everyday thinking?

Ethics

Knowledge confers power—and responsibility. Should everything we learn be shared? What duties do we owe to those affected by our claims? TOK explores issues of plagiarism, misinformation, intellectual property, and the moral obligations that arise when our knowledge can harm or help others.
