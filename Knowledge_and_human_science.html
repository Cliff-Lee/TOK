Knowledge & the Human Sciences

Exploring how we study ourselves through Theory of Knowledge lenses

Scope

The scope of the human sciences sets out what counts as valid inquiry when we investigate people and societies. It asks: What questions can we realistically answer about human behaviour? Consider how disciplines such as psychology, economics, and anthropology define their objects of study, build explanatory models, and set boundaries between descriptive facts and value‑laden judgments.

Perspectives

Perspectives emphasise that our findings in the human sciences are never neutral; they are filtered through cultural lenses, theoretical paradigms, and researcher biases. Ask yourself: Whose viewpoint shapes the data we collect, and how might alternative perspectives change the story?

Methods & Tools

The methods and tools of human‑science research range from controlled experiments to big‑data analytics and ethnography. Key TOK questions include: How do different methods affect reliability? What ethical limits guide our choice of tools?

Ethics

Ethics confronts the responsibilities that arise when we research real people. It challenges us to balance the pursuit of knowledge with respect for autonomy, privacy, and justice. Consider: When does benefit outweigh harm, and who gets to decide?
