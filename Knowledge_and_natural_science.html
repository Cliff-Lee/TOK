TOK – Knowledge & the Natural Sciences

Scope

The natural sciences aim to develop testable explanations and universal laws that describe the physical world—from sub‑atomic particles to the evolution of galaxies. In TOK we ask how far those aims can succeed, what counts as a legitimate scientific question, and where the boundaries lie between science, pseudoscience, and other ways of knowing.

Methods & Tools

Scientific knowledge is built through systematic observation, controlled experimentation, mathematical modelling, and peer review. TOK invites us to probe the reliability of these methods: How do instruments extend (and limit) our senses? How do statistics and computer simulations shape what we count as evidence? When do scientific revolutions overturn accepted "facts"?

Perspectives

Although science seeks objectivity, it is practised by communities situated in particular cultures, funding structures, and historical moments. TOK explores how perspective influences research questions, data interpretation, and paradigm shifts—from Newtonian mechanics to quantum physics. We consider the value of interdisciplinary and Indigenous perspectives in broadening scientific understanding.

Ethics

Scientific advances raise ethical issues: the use of human or animal subjects, environmental impacts of research, dual‑use technologies such as nuclear energy or gene editing, and questions of bias or data privacy. TOK challenges us to decide when the pursuit of knowledge should be limited, and who gets to set those limits.
